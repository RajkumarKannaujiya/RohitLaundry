Frequently Asked Questions

Optimized for quick answers regarding our fabric care processes.

What is the difference between dry cleaning and petrol wash?
Dry cleaning uses standard chemical solvents, while Petrol Wash uses milder hydrocarbon solvents. Petrol wash is ultra-gentle and specifically reserved for highly embellished ethnic wear like heavy Zari Sarees, Bridal Lehengas, and garments with delicate mirror work to prevent dulling the embellishments.
Can you remove tough stains like oil, ink, or turmeric?
Yes. Our spotting experts use specialized chemical agents to treat specific stains like ink, turmeric, grease, and wine before the main wash. While we have a very high success rate, the outcome depends on the age of the stain and whether it was previously treated improperly with water at home.
How do you handle commercial Spa and Salon towels?
Spa towels require specialized treatment to break down heavy massage oils that cause rancid odors. We use industrial-grade emulsifiers and high-temperature thermal washes to completely strip the oils, leaving the towels hygienic, soft, and fresh smelling for your clients.
Is steam pressing safe for heavy embroidered Kurtis?
Absolutely. Normal dry iron plates flatten and melt embroidery threads. Our industrial steam press uses a vacuum board; the garment is laid flat, steam is injected to relax the fibers, and the vacuum pulls it crisp. This "soft-press" technique removes wrinkles without ever applying crushing pressure to the embroidery.
